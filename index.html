Marian House Redesign

Redesigning a non-profits website to increase transparency among community members

About the project

Role

Project manager

Marian Houses serves homeless women and their children in the Penn Lucy and Better Waverly neighborhoods in Baltimore, MD. Along with transitional and permanent housing, Marian House offers services in counseling, education, and employment.

Tools

Figma FigJam Userberry

We worked with stakeholders and potential users to redesign their site to better cater to the organizations goals, along with user desires. We worked with stakeholders and community users to determine a goal for our redesign: 1) Build trust between Marian House and community members

Duration

8 weeks

I led a team of three other user researchers as we conducted qualitative research and stakeholder interviews. Through analysis we developed a user personas for which we brainstormed solutions for their pain points. We then implemented these solutions and tested them using Figma and Useberry for prototype testing. We continued iterating and testing our prototype until we had a high fidelity prototype.

Year
