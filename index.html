Marian House Redesign

Redesigning a non-profits website to increase transparency among community members

About the project

Role

Project manager

Marian Houses serves homeless women and their children in the Penn Lucy and Better Waverly neighborhoods in Baltimore, MD. Along with transitional and permanent housing, Marian House offers services in counseling, education, and employment.

Tools

Figma FigJam Userberry

We worked with stakeholders and potential users to redesign their site to better cater to the organizations goals, along with user desires. We worked with stakeholders and community users to determine a goal for our redesign: 1) Build trust between Marian House and community members

Duration

8 weeks

I led a team of three other user researchers as we conducted qualitative research and stakeholder interviews. Through analysis we developed a user personas for which we brainstormed solutions for their pain points. We then implemented these solutions and tested them using Figma and Useberry for prototype testing. We continued iterating and testing our prototype until we had a high fidelity prototype.

Year

2022

  • Competitive analysis

    We conducted a competitor analysis and found no consistency in non-profit web presence. This may be due to tight budgets and limited staff. We did not find a competitor analysis to be helpful in our research, and opted instead for literary reviews of non-proft we presence recommendations [as seen in our user flow (v.1)]

  • User Interview and Test

    We conducted several 1x1 interviews with users, along with facilitating user tests on the Marian House donation flow. All of our interviewees have expereince donating to a non-profit, 75%Â have experience with online giving. The following insightes were gained:

      Users felt the donation page looked unsecure
      Users felt there were too many steps to get to the donation page
      Users would like to see a donation breakdown
      Something that would demonstrate the impact of their donation
    The key takeaway from our users interviews was one specific goal:
    Users would opt out of donating to Marian House due to lack of trust for the organization
  • Stakeholder Interview

    The stakeholder already had a good idea of what they wanted to do, and gladly discussed with us the direction they saw the site going. The intended audience of their site are community members and potential donors.
    The stakeholder expressed discouragement that the current homepage did not present the Marian House mission and value statement well. We discussed the following concerns with them:

      While the intended audience of the site is donors, they see very few donations coming in through the site
      Marian House serves a large demographic of women in a myriad of areas, how can we efficiently and clearly demonstrate this on the sites homepage?
      Their current site is designed and managed by an outside organization, one the stakeholder feels does not truly understand the heart of Marian House.
    We determined the following stakeholder goals:
      The design will better cater to intended audience (donors and community members)
      The homepage will deliver a clear message of what Marian House is about.
  • User Persona
    We developed a user persona to reflect the insights we found during our interview process and affinity mapping.
  • Storyboard
    We produced a story board to reflect our users experience with our web design.
  • Feature Prioritization
    Our feature prioritzation helps us narrow our focus to three key features
  • SWOT Analysis
    Our SWOT analysis helped us look at the whole picture of our prorject.
